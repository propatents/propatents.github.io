Case for patents

1. Encourages innovation

2. Enriches the innovator

3. Motivates the innovator

4. Patents defend innovative ideas

Innovative ideas like Amazon's one click buy patent 1,2 which pushes the envelope of the technology is defended for Amazon only by the patent system. But there are many more innovative ideas which need a defense by laws, here's a sampling 3:

  • U.S. Patent 6080436: toasting bread in a toaster operating between 2500 and 4500 degrees. 4
  • U.S. Patent 6004596: the sealed crustless peanut butter and jelly sandwich. 5
  • U.S. Patent 5616089: a "putting method in which the golfer controls the speed of the putt and the direction of the putt primarily with the golfer’s dominant throwing hand, yet uses the golfer’s nondominant hand to maintain the blade of the putter stable." 6
  • U.S. Patent 6368227: "A method of swing on a swing is disclosed, in which a user positioned on a standard swing suspended by two chains from a substantially horizontal tree branch induces side to side motion by pulling alternately on one chain and then the other." 7
  • U.S. Patent 6293874: "User-operated amusement apparatus for kicking the user's buttocks" 8
  • U.S. Patent D670286: a portable device with rounded rounded corners. 9

Silly and obvious inventions like SpaceX's rockets are not patented.10 Because of this SpaceX will soon go bankrupt after the imitators start to copy their inventions, as every other company which has not patented its ideas, like:

  • TravelPro, the inventor of Rollaboard, "a vertical bag with two wheels and an extendable handle that one could roll through airports and carry on airplanes". 11
  • Ferrero, the owner of the Nutella hazelnut chocolate spread. 12
  • Pepsi, the first company with light-weight, recyclable, plastic bottles. 13, 14

5. Patents are cheap

6. The patent system creates jobs

7. The pharmaceutical industry cannot exists without patents

8. Patent laws improve over time
